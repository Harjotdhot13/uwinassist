APPLICATION FOR ADMISSION

Application fees is -$105

FORMS NEEDED

The Confidential Report Forms should be forwarded to your referees. The Confidential Report forms should be returned by the referees directly to the Office of the Registrar, Graduate Studies Division, University of Windsor, Windsor, ON, N9B 3P4 or returned to the student in a sealed envelope. The forms are included on the on-line application.

Transcripts

Applicants who have attended an Ontario University must order a transcript when applying on-line. Applicants who have attended universities outside of Ontario must order transcripts directly from the institution. Please request from each university which you attended official transcripts to be sent directly to the Office of the Registrar-Graduate Studies Division or sent to the applicant and returned to the University unopened. Applicants who have attended the University of Windsor need not order or send Windsor transcripts. All documents received become the property of the University of Windsor and will not be returned. You may submit copies of official transcripts, but if an offer of admission is extended on the basis of unofficial transcripts, final official transcripts must be submitted prior to registration in the program.

Accepting your offer.

Accept your offer by returning the completed confirmation form sent along with your Acceptance Response Form to intladmit@uwindsor.ca.

Pay your deposit.

In order to study in Canada as an International Student, Immigration Canada requires proof of financial support you have for yourself and your accompany family members when you are in Canda. If you want to deposit your tution and fees with the University of Windsor to meet this requirement, you should:
a) be informed of any Immigration Canada's requirements;
b) choose one of the payment options provided to international students;
c)Once the University receives the money, it will be credited to your student account at the University.

Apply for study permit

• International students will require a Study Permit to study in Canada for longer than six months. Depending on the country you are from, you may also need to acquire a Temporary Resident Visa to enter Canada.
• To find out how to apply for a Study Permit/Temporary Resident Visa, please visit Citizenship and Immigration Canada's website.
2. Submit your study permit application
We strongly advise you to apply for your Study Permit/Temporary Resident Visa as soon as you accept your offer, as the process may take from a couple of weeks to several months, depending on where and when you apply.
3. Inform after the result of application
Please keep us updated with your Study Permit/TRV application results so that we will know if we should expect to see you on campus.

Apply For Graduation

All students must apply to graduate regardless of attendance at the Convocation Ceremony.
The deadlines to apply to graduate and the associated fee to attend the ceremony are:
March 1st for the Spring convocation at $40
August 1st for the Fall convocation held in October at $40
Late applications to attend the ceremony, and the associated fee, will be accepted from
March 2nd to one month prior to the Spring ceremony at $80
August 2nd to one month prior to the Fall ceremony at $80
To apply to graduate, please log on to myUWindsor and click on the Academics tab and use the Apply to Graduate link under Academic Tools.
No applications will be accepted within 4 weeks prior to convocation.
